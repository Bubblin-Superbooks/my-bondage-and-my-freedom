I have never heard any songs like those anywhere since I left slavery, except when in Ireland. There I heard the same wailing notes, and was much affected by them. It was during the famine of 1845-6. In all the songs of the slaves, there was ever some expression in praise of the great house farm; something which would flatter the pride of the owner, and, possibly, draw a favorable glance from him.

      _I am going away to the great house farm,
      O yea!  O yea!  O yea!
      My old master is a good old master,
      O yea!  O yea!  O yea!_
